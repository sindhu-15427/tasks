Text formatting tags in HTML are used to make text more readable, attractive, and meaningful. They help highlight important words, show emphasis, and organize content better.

Common Formatting Tags

  • b: — Makes text bold.
  • i: — Makes text italic.
  • u: — Underlines text.
  • mark: — Highlights text.
  • small: — Makes text smaller.
  • sub: — Displays text as subscript (e.g., H2O).
  • sup: — Displays text as superscript (e.g., X2).
  • strong: — Indicates important text (bold with meaning).
  • em: — Emphasizes text (italic with meaning).

Example of Formatting:

This is bold text, italic text, and underlined text.
You can also highlight important text.
Water formula: H2O and math formula: X2.
Use strong for important meaning and emphasis for stress.

Example Uses:

  • Highlighting important points in an article.
  • Displaying chemical or mathematical formulas.
  • Emphasizing words for better understanding.

Summary:

Text formatting helps make web content more structured and visually appealing. It enhances readability and draws attention to key information.
